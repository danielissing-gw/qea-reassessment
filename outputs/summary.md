# QEA Reassessment Summary

## Overview

Reassessed 13 GiveWell QEAs (quick evidence assessments) to identify interventions worth revisiting, based on changes in evidence, costs, burden, funding, or technology since the original assessment.

**Results:** 1 High, 4 Medium, 8 Low.

| Rating | Count | Interventions |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| High | 1 | Rheumatic heart disease prevention |
| Medium | 4 | Uterotonics for PPH, Oral azithromycin during labor, Antenatal corticosteroids for preterm birth, (+ PPH at borderline) |
| Low | 8 | ProCCM, Ponseti clubfoot, Diabetes prevention (K-DPP), Flood-tolerant rice, Road traffic safety legislation, Phone audits, ANC CCTs, Rabies dog vaccination, LPG cookstoves |

---

## High priority

### Rheumatic heart disease prevention
- **Original CE:** 58.6x | **Updated CE:** ~26x (range 16–54x)
- WHO published first formal clinical guideline on RF/RHD in Nov 2024. GBD 2021 shows India burden ~29% higher than assumed. 2023 Lancet India CEA finds secondary prevention cost-effective at ~$30/QALY. Cost remains the pivotal unknown — BOTEC includes 3x subjective cost multiplier.
- **Next step:** Obtain real programmatic cost data for secondary prevention in India.

---

## Medium priority

### Uterotonics for PPH prevention
- **Original CE:** Not calculated | **Updated CE:** ~3x (range 2–7x)
- TXA now WHO-recommended for PPH treatment (WOMAN trial RR 0.81). Heat-stable carbetocin is a new WHO-recommended product ($0.31/ampoule). CE highly sensitive to per-birth cost — at $1/birth reaches ~6.5x. AMPLI-PPHI cost data (expected mid-2026) would resolve key uncertainty.
- **Next step:** Wait for Unitaid AMPLI-PPHI programmatic cost data.

### Oral azithromycin during labor
- **Original CE:** ~12x (threshold) | **Updated CE:** ~4x at $3/pregnancy (range 2–9x)
- A-PLUS trial (NEJM 2023) found RR 0.67 for maternal sepsis/death but null neonatal result. Neonatal mortality drove 66–86% of original CE — now zero. Maternal effect is 4x larger than assumed but applies to few deaths. CE reaches 8x+ only at very low delivery costs ($1.50/pregnancy).
- **Next step:** Get delivery cost estimates from A-PLUS PI (Dr. Alan Tita); consider bundling with PPH interventions.

### Antenatal corticosteroids for preterm birth
- **Original CE:** ~28x (crude) | **Updated CE:** ~14x at $30/woman (range 6–39x)
- ACTION-I trial (NEJM 2020) resolved the key concern from the ACT trial — ACS reduces neonatal death by 16% (RR 0.84) when properly targeted in LMIC hospitals. WHO updated guidelines in 2022. Cost-saving in all 5 trial countries. Massive implementation gap (~10% of facilities provide ACS).
- **Next step:** Obtain implementation cost estimates for ACS scale-up from WHO ACTION team; monitor ACTION-III results (late preterm, expected ~2027).

## Cross-cutting observations

1. **Facility-based maternal/neonatal interventions cluster together.** Three of four Medium-rated interventions (oral azithromycin, ACS, uterotonics) are single-dose or short-course drugs given during labor/delivery at health facilities. Shared delivery infrastructure could reduce per-intervention costs if bundled. Worth investigating the AMPLI-PPHI platform as a potential vehicle.

2. **Cost data is the binding constraint for most promising interventions.** For RHD, PPH, oral azithromycin, and ACS, the CE estimate is highly sensitive to program delivery costs, and actual programmatic cost data is either unavailable or outdated. Getting real cost data from implementers would move several of these from "plausible" to "confirmed" or "ruled out."

3. **The evidence base has improved substantially since the original QEAs.** Major RCTs have reported for ACS (ACTION-I), oral azithromycin (A-PLUS), PPH interventions (WOMAN trial, E-MOTIVE), and cookstoves (HAPIN). WHO has updated guidelines for ACS (2022), RHD (2024), and PPH drugs. This makes the current assessment substantially more grounded than the originals.

4. **Several interventions were ruled out by other actors' investigations.** GiveWell independently investigated clubfoot (funding MiracleFeet), PxD (scoping grant), and clean cookstoves (concluding not a priority). Bloomberg covers road safety. GAVI entered rabies PEP. This validates the QEA screening approach but reduces the marginal value of further investigation for those interventions.
