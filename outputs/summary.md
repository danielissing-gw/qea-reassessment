# QEA Reassessment Summary

## Overview

Reassessed 33 GiveWell QEAs (quick evidence assessments) to identify interventions worth revisiting, based on changes in evidence, costs, burden, funding, or technology since the original assessment.

**Results:** 1 High, 6 Medium, 26 Low.

## High priority

### Rheumatic heart disease prevention
- **Original CE:** 58.6x | **Updated CE:** ~26x (range 16–54x)
- WHO published first formal clinical guideline on RF/RHD in Nov 2024. GBD 2021 shows India burden ~29% higher than assumed. 2023 Lancet India CEA finds secondary prevention cost-effective at ~$30/QALY. Cost remains the pivotal unknown — BOTEC includes 3x subjective cost multiplier.
- **Next step:** Obtain real programmatic cost data for secondary prevention in India.

## Medium priority

### Uterotonics for PPH prevention
- **Original CE:** Not calculated | **Updated CE:** ~3x (range 2–7x)
- TXA now WHO-recommended for PPH treatment (WOMAN trial RR 0.81). Heat-stable carbetocin is a new WHO-recommended product ($0.31/ampoule). CE highly sensitive to per-birth cost — at $1/birth reaches ~6.5x. AMPLI-PPHI cost data (expected mid-2026) would resolve key uncertainty.
- **Next step:** Wait for Unitaid AMPLI-PPHI programmatic cost data.

### Oral azithromycin during labor
- **Original CE:** ~12x (threshold) | **Updated CE:** ~4x at $3/pregnancy (range 2–9x)
- A-PLUS trial (NEJM 2023) found RR 0.67 for maternal sepsis/death but null neonatal result. Neonatal mortality drove 66–86% of original CE — now zero. Maternal effect is 4x larger than assumed but applies to few deaths. CE reaches 8x+ only at very low delivery costs ($1.50/pregnancy).
- **Next step:** Get delivery cost estimates from A-PLUS PI (Dr. Alan Tita); consider bundling with PPH interventions.

### Antenatal corticosteroids for preterm birth
- **Original CE:** ~28x (crude) | **Updated CE:** ~14x at $30/woman (range 6–39x)
- ACTION-I trial (NEJM 2020) resolved the key concern from the ACT trial — ACS reduces neonatal death by 16% (RR 0.84) when properly targeted in LMIC hospitals. WHO updated guidelines in 2022. Cost-saving in all 5 trial countries. Massive implementation gap (~10% of facilities provide ACS).
- **Next step:** Obtain implementation cost estimates for ACS scale-up from WHO ACTION team; monitor ACTION-III results (late preterm, expected ~2027).

### Non-pneumatic anti-shock garment (NASG)
- **Original CE:** ~15x at $8/woman; ~5x at CHAI costs | **Updated CE:** ~10x at $12/woman (range 5–15x)
- Evidence base unchanged (pooled RR 0.52 from 5 observational studies + 1 underpowered RCT; no new RCTs feasible). But CHAI Zimbabwe pilot (2019–2021) showed strong real-world results: 90% device usage, 50% fewer maternal deaths, zero PPH deaths across 34 facilities. Zimbabwe MOHCC adopted NASG for national rollout. Zambia also scaling (2023 process evaluation published).
- **Next step:** Obtain actual cost-per-woman data from CHAI Zimbabwe (Andrew Storey); investigate bundling with E-MOTIVE PPH protocol and uterotonics.

### Sayana Press injectable contraceptive
- **Original CE:** ~$88,700/death averted (mortality channel) | **Updated CE:** ~13x at $12/woman-year (range 7–35x)
- GiveWell published a contraception valuation framework (April 2025): 0.7 UoV per woman-year of modern contraception (range 0.3–1.1). This directly resolves the moral weight blocker from the 2016 QEA. Self-injection now approved in 35+ countries and available in 55+ countries. Meta-analysis of 3 RCTs: self-injection increases 12-month continuation by 27% (RR 1.27). Commodity cost locked at $1/dose via Pfizer/Gates/CIFF agreement.
- **Next step:** Get delivery cost estimates for self-injection programs from PATH/PSI; assess GiveWell's current family planning investigation pipeline.

### MMS during pregnancy
- **Original CE:** ~7x (range 2–14x) | **Updated CE:** ~9x at $3/pregnancy (range 5–13x)
- WHO included UNIMMAP MMS on Essential Medicines List (2021). Kirk Humanitarian committed $125M and reached 75M pregnant women in 111 LMICs at $0.0118/dose ($2.13/pregnancy). 2025 meta-analyses confirm benefits extend beyond birth: stunting (RR 0.86) and underweight (RR 0.86) through 24 months. Copenhagen Consensus 2023 named MMS one of 12 best investments ($37 return per $1).
- **Next step:** Request disaggregated cost-per-pregnancy data from UNICEF's MMS program; investigate whether GW's early-life growth work can anchor the moral weight for LBW.

## Cross-cutting observations

1. **Facility-based maternal/neonatal interventions cluster together.** Four of six Medium-rated interventions (oral azithromycin, ACS, uterotonics, NASG) are facility-based interventions for labor/delivery. Shared delivery infrastructure could reduce per-intervention costs if bundled. Worth investigating the AMPLI-PPHI platform and E-MOTIVE protocol as potential vehicles.

2. **Cost data is the binding constraint for most promising interventions.** For RHD, PPH, oral azithromycin, ACS, NASG, Sayana Press, and MMS, the CE estimate is highly sensitive to program delivery costs, and actual programmatic cost data is either unavailable or outdated. Getting real cost data from implementers would move several of these from "plausible" to "confirmed" or "ruled out."

3. **The evidence base has improved substantially since the original QEAs.** Major RCTs have reported for ACS (ACTION-I), oral azithromycin (A-PLUS), PPH interventions (WOMAN trial, E-MOTIVE), cookstoves (HAPIN), drowning (PRECISE), salt substitution (SSaSS), WASH/HAI (APT-Sepsis), and rape prevention (newer Kenya RCT). WHO has updated guidelines for ACS (2022), RHD (2024), PPH drugs, HIV self-testing (2022-23), salt substitution (2025), and MMS (2020-21). This makes the current assessment substantially more grounded than the originals.

4. **Several interventions were ruled out by other actors' investigations.** GiveWell independently investigated clubfoot (funding MiracleFeet), PxD (scoping grant), and clean cookstoves (concluding not a priority). Bloomberg covers road safety and drowning. GAVI entered rabies PEP. PEPFAR/Global Fund fund HIV self-testing. Kirk Humanitarian scaled MMS to 75M women. This validates the QEA screening approach but reduces the marginal value of further investigation for those interventions.

5. **A comprehensive PPH package merits investigation.** Three interventions address different parts of PPH: prevention (heat-stable carbetocin), treatment (TXA + NASG), and early detection (E-MOTIVE drape). Oral azithromycin (maternal sepsis prevention) could also be bundled on the same facility platform. If any of these interventions were to be funded, a unified assessment of the full package would be more informative than individual analyses.

6. **GiveWell's contraception valuation unlocks two previously blocked interventions.** GW's April 2025 contraception valuation (0.7 UoV per woman-year) directly resolves the moral weight blocker for Sayana Press and is relevant to DMI radio campaigns. Sayana Press becomes clearly promising (~13x) with self-injection driving down costs. GW's active investigation of family planning suggests this space may already be on their radar.

7. **MMS may be the strongest "new" finding.** MMS during pregnancy was rated Medium in the original 2021 QEA (~7x, uncertain costs). Since then, Kirk Humanitarian's $125M commitment has driven commodity costs to $2.13/pregnancy, WHO added MMS to the Essential Medicines List, and 2025 meta-analyses confirm benefits beyond birth (stunting, underweight). At updated costs (~$3/pregnancy), CE reaches ~9x. The remaining uncertainty is programmatic costs — but with 75M women already reached across 111 LMICs, real-world data should exist.
